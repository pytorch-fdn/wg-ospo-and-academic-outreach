# Chairs and Co-Chairs

Chairs and Co-Chairs are responsible for coordinating and running the working group, including setting meeting agendas, facilitating discussions, tracking action items, and helping ensure progress against the group’s goals and deliverables.

The Chair also serves as the primary liaison between the working group, the PyTorch Foundation, and relevant Linux Foundation resources when support, guidance, or coordination is needed. This may include, for example, identifying opportunities for students to present at conferences and webinars or engage with PyTorch Foundation certifications, trainings, and other relevant programs.

## Appointment

A Chair or Co-Chair may step into the role by demonstrating sustained engagement in the working group and following the governance process agreed upon by the working group.

## Stepping Down

A Chair or Co-Chair may step down by notifying the working group and the PyTorch Foundation Staff. The role may also be ended or reassigned if the Chair or Co-Chair is no 
longer able to fulfill the responsibilities, if the working group agrees that a change is needed, or if the PyTorch Foundation determines that a transition is necessary.
