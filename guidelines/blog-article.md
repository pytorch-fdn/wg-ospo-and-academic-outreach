# Academic Blog Guidelines for the PyTorch OSPO and Academic Outreach Working Group

## Purpose

When Working Group members publish academic blog posts through PyTorch Foundation channels, they should not only showcase a project or research outcome, but also strengthen awareness of the PyTorch OSPO and Academic Outreach and encourage broader community participation

## Guidelines

### 1. Connect the work to the broader academic community

Frame the project as an example of a broader challenge, practice, or opportunity within academia, rather than an isolated success story using PyTorch ecosystem projects

Examples:

* Open source education and curriculum
* Research software sustainability
* Student contributions
* Academic OSPOs
* Reproducibility and open science
* University–industry collaboration

### 2. Acknowledge the academic origin

Where appropriate, mention:

* University or research institution
* Course, lab, or research group
* Student or faculty contributors
* How the work evolved into an open source contribution

### 3. Include a Working Group call to action

Every academic-focused post should end with a short CTA inviting readers to participate

Example:

> Interested in advancing open source in research and education? Join the PyTorch Academic OSPO Working Group to collaborate with universities, researchers, and open source practitioners on growing the academic PyTorch ecosystem

### 4. Highlight opportunities for collaboration

Whenever possible, identify areas where the broader community can contribute, such as:

* Sharing similar academic projects
* Providing feedback
* Adopting the project
* Contributing code or documentation
* Participating in Working Group discussions

### 5. Showcase reusable lessons

Go beyond describing the project by sharing insights other institutions can apply:

* Challenges faced
* Best practices
* Lessons learned
* Recommendations for similar initiatives

### 6. Use consistent Working Group branding

Where appropriate:

* Mention the PyTorch Academic OSPO Working Group by name and logo
* Link to the Working Group repo

#### Suggested Blog Structure

1. Problem or research challenge
2. The academic project
3. Technical implementation
4. Impact and lessons learned
5. Opportunities for community collaboration
6. Call to action to join the Academic OSPO Working Group

#### Example Closing CTA

This project is one example of how universities are advancing the PyTorch ecosystem through open collaboration
If you’re interested in sharing academic projects, developing best practices, or connecting with peers working at the intersection of PyTorch and academia, consider joining the PyTorch Academic OSPO Working Group
We welcome researchers, educators, students, and open source practitioners interested in building this community together
