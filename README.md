# OSPO and Academic Outreach Working Group

This repository supports the PyTorch Foundation OSPO and Academic Outreach Working Group. The working group exists to help shape and support efforts that:

- strengthen open source AI capability in academia,
- support students, researchers, and faculty in releasing their projects to open source using stronger governance, licensing, security, and community practices,
- help academia educate foundations about the obligations and constraints under which academic researchers work, and
- increase academic adoption of and contribution to PyTorch Foundation-hosted projects while improving the pathway for academic open source projects to enter the PyTorch Ecosystem and, where appropriate, progress toward foundation-hosted candidacy

## Charter

The working group charter defines the mission, goals, scope, operating model, deliverables, and success criteria for this effort. [Read the Charter](./CHARTER.md) to learn about the expected goals, roles, deliverables, workflow, and operating model.

## Goals

As established in the charter, this working group is centered on three primary goals:

### 1. Open source AI capability uplift in academia

Support academia in developing open source AI practices by helping researchers, students, faculty, labs, and university open source offices better understand and adopt:

- governance practices,
- licensing and IP considerations,
- software supply chain security practices,
- community operations, and
- sustainable maintainer models

### 2. Adoption and contribution to PyTorch Foundation-hosted projects

Support academic usage of and contribution to PyTorch Foundation-hosted projects, including:

- PyTorch,
- vLLM,
- Ray,
- DeepSpeed and
- Any future hosted projects

### 3. Ecosystem and hosting pipeline for academic projects

Support the development of a clearer and more consistent path for academic open source projects to:

- improve baseline open source readiness,
- become part of the PyTorch Ecosystem, and
- prepare for foundation-hosted project candidacy through existing PyTorch Foundation processes.

## Expected Output Materials

In alignment with the charter, the working group may develop and maintain materials related to:

- academic open source AI readiness,
- technical enablement for PyTorch Foundation-hosted projects,
- readiness reviews and project intake support,
- ecosystem pathway guidance,
- mentorship and review frameworks,
- workshops, office hours, and educational materials,
- directory and outreach design to support collaboration across academia and the PyTorch Foundation ecosystem

## Repository Contents

This repository may be used to organize and maintain:

- the working group charter,
- meeting notes,

- training and workshop materials,
- readiness checklists and templates,
- and more

## Who Should Participate

Participating in the working group is open to anyone, regardless of their organization's membership status with PyTorch Foundation. This working group may be relevant to:

- students,
- academic researchers,
- university OSPOs,
- maintainers of academic open source projects,
- PyTorch Foundation contributors and maintainers,
- mentors and reviewers supporting academic open source development
