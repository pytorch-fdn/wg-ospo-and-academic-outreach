# Curriculum Development Track (CDT)

## Purpose

The Curriculum Development Track (CDT) is an operational workstream under the PyTorch Foundation OSPO & Academic Outreach Working Group. It is responsible for designing, producing, and delivering educational materials that help academic communities adopt open source AI practices and contribute to PyTorch Foundation-hosted projects.

CDT takes a research-informed, first-principles approach: every piece of content starts from understanding what academic audiences actually need (via surveys and listening sessions), then moves through progressively deeper material — from student-friendly blogs and starter kits to modular research-grade curriculum.

## Relationship to Charter

CDT is the execution arm for two charter tracks:

| Charter Track | CDT Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Track A — Academic Open Source AI Readiness** | Produce the modular curriculum, governance templates, and workshop content defined in Track A |
| **Track B — PyTorch Foundation Projects Enablement** | Develop project-specific workshop kits, guest lecture materials, and contribution pathway guides for PyTorch, vLLM, DeepSpeed, and Ray |

CDT also supports **Track C (Academic Fast Track)** by producing the readiness review checklists and mentorship materials that projects use during the intake pipeline.

## Regional Focus: APAC Expansion

The initial CDT effort targets the **Asia-Pacific academic open source AI community**, with anchor partnerships at:

### India
- **IIT campuses** — Student clubs and faculty with active AI/ML and open source engagement (IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Madras, IIT Kharagpur, and others)
- **IISc Bangalore** — Research labs in Computational and Data Sciences (CDS), Computer Science and Automation (CSA), and related departments

### Singapore
- **Student-led AI/ML clubs** — At institutions including NUS, NTU, SUTD, and SMU
- **AI Singapore ecosystem** — Community programs and practitioner networks

Future CDT cohorts may expand to other APAC regions, EMEA, and the Americas based on demand and WG capacity.

## Methodology

CDT follows a three-phase approach for each regional cohort:

```
Phase 1: Survey & Discovery
        │
        │   Understand what the audience actually needs
        │   before building anything.
        │
        ▼
Phase 2: Student-Friendly Content
        │
        │   Approachable blogs, starter kits, and visual
        │   guides that meet students where they are.
        │
        ▼
Phase 3: First-Principles Research Materials
        │
        │   Modular, deep curriculum for researchers
        │   and graduate students aligned to charter
        │   Track A and Track B modules.
        │
        ▼
    Feedback loop → refine → next cohort
```

### Phase 1 — Survey & Discovery

Understand the current state of open source engagement, barriers, tooling awareness, and content preferences across target institutions before producing any materials.

**Activities:**
- Design and distribute an academic open source readiness survey
- Map the regional ecosystem: labs, clubs, OSPOs, faculty champions, active projects
- Identify anchor contacts willing to champion WG engagement locally
- Analyze results and publish a summary report (shared back with respondents)

**Output:** Survey results report, APAC ecosystem map, identified pilot cohort

### Phase 2 — Student-Friendly Content

Produce lightweight, practical content that builds awareness and gives students something they can use immediately.

**Activities:**
- Publish a blog series covering open source fundamentals for AI students
- Create an "Academic Open Source Starter Kit" (template repo, checklists, quick-reference guides)
- Identify student co-authors from target institutions (authorship credit as an incentive)

**Output:** Blog series (5 posts), starter kit template repo, release checklist, visual quick-reference card

### Phase 3 — First-Principles Research Materials

Develop deeper, modular curriculum for graduate students and faculty, aligned to the charter's Track A core modules.

**Activities:**
- Produce self-contained curriculum modules (learning objectives, reading material, hands-on exercises, self-assessment)
- Cover: project design, governance models, reproducibility standards, supply chain security, upstream contribution pathways
- Pilot with one institution before wider release

**Output:** 5 curriculum modules, reviewer feedback from pilot cohort, iteration plan

## Scope

### In scope

- Survey design, distribution, and analysis for target academic audiences
- Blog posts, guides, and visual materials for students new to open source
- Modular curriculum aligned to charter Track A and Track B modules
- Template repositories and governance document templates for academic projects
- Regional ecosystem mapping (labs, clubs, OSPOs, contacts) feeding into charter Track D directory
- Coordination with PyTorch Foundation marketing team for blog publishing and speaker scheduling
- Identifying and onboarding student co-authors and regional champions

### Out of scope

- Hosting or running academic courses for credit (CDT produces materials; institutions deliver them)
- Translating materials into local languages (may be considered in future phases)
- Direct management of academic projects (covered by charter Track C — Academic Fast Track)
- Funding decisions for academic institutions or projects

## Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **CDT Lead** | Overall coordination of the workstream; owns the phased roadmap and delivery timeline |
| **Survey Coordinator** | Designs, distributes, and analyzes the readiness survey; produces the results report |
| **Content Authors** | Write blog posts, guides, and curriculum modules; may include student co-authors |
| **Regional Champions** | On-the-ground contacts at target institutions who help with survey distribution, pilot coordination, and feedback |
| **Reviewers** | WG members and PyTorch Foundation project maintainers who review materials for accuracy and alignment |
